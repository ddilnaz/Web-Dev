Recipe #1: Pizza

  • Time: 1 hour
  • Serves: Lunch/Dinner
Ingredients Quantity
1pc (300-400g)
Water 500ml
bread flour 3/4 cups (490g)
Cheese 200g
tomato 4-5

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 220 degrees Celsius (425 degrees Fahrenheit).
  2. Roll out the pizza dough on a baking sheet and spread tomato sauce evenly.
  3. Sprinkle with cheese, add sliced tomatoes, ham (or pepperoni), and olives.
  4. Sprinkle with Italian herbs if desired.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for about 15-20 minutes or until the crust is golden brown.
  6. Remove the pizza from the oven, let it cool for a bit, slice, and enjoy!

Recipe Burger

  • Time: 60 min
  • Serves: Dinner
Ingredients Quantity
Flour 300-400g
Water 500ml
Chicken 400g
Salad 100g
Tomato 200g

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the grill or stovetop pan.
  2. Divide the ground beef into patties and season with salt and pepper.
  3. Cook the patties on the grill or pan until they reach your preferred level of doneness.
  4. Toast the burger buns on the grill or in a toaster.
  5. Assemble the burgers by placing lettuce, a cooked patty, tomato slices, onion slices, and cheese (if using) on the bun.
  6. Spread ketchup, mustard, and mayonnaise on the top bun and add pickle slices.
  7. Place the top bun over the filling to complete the burger.
  8. Serve the burgers with your favorite sides and enjoy!

Kimchi

  • Time: 50 min min
  • Serves: Breakfast/Lunch/Dinner
Ingredients Quantity
Cabbage 1kg
species a lot

Instructions:

  1. Cut the Napa cabbage into quarters lengthwise and remove the core. Cut into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Julienne the daikon radish and carrots. Slice the green onions.
  3. Mince garlic cloves and ginger.
  4. In a large bowl, mix the vegetables with salt. Let it sit for a couple of hours to allow the vegetables to release water.
  5. Rinse the vegetables to remove excess salt. Drain well.
  6. In a separate bowl, make a paste by mixing red pepper flakes, minced garlic, minced ginger, fish sauce, soy sauce, and sugar.
  7. Combine the paste with the drained vegetables, ensuring they are well-coated.
  8. Pack the kimchi mixture into a clean, airtight jar, pressing it down to remove air bubbles.
  9. Seal the jar and let it ferment at room temperature for a day or two, then refrigerate.
  10. After a few days, your homemade kimchi is ready to be enjoyed!
